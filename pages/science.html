Science Stream GPA Calculator FAQs

Answers to common questions about Class 11 & 12 Science stream grading.

NEB Science stream includes: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics (compulsory), plus optional subjects like Computer Science, Optional Mathematics, or additional sciences. Each subject has different credit hours and theory-practical weightings. Use this calculator to input all your subjects for accurate GPA.

In Science, practical work is weighted by subject: Physics & Chemistry (50% theory / 50% practical), Biology (40% theory / 60% practical), and Mathematics (100% theory). This calculator automatically applies the correct weighting for each subject based on NEB standards.

A 3.6+ GPA is considered excellent in Science stream and is competitive for university admissions and scholarships. A 3.2+ is considered good, while 2.8-3.2 is average. For medical and engineering entrance exams, 3.6+ is typically preferred. Check specific university requirements for admission cutoffs.

Yes. All NEB streams use the same 4.0 GPA scale, so GPAs are directly comparable across streams. However, subject combinations differ by stream. Science focuses on sciences and maths, while Management includes business subjects. The grading scale and difficulty vary, so context matters for fair comparison.

Class 11 and Class 12 Science use the same subject structure and GPA calculation method. The main difference is curriculum depth and exam difficulty—Class 12 builds on Class 11 concepts. This calculator works for both levels. If you want to track both, calculate each year separately for year-wise comparison.

Focus on weak subjects first—improving one subject grade significantly impacts overall GPA due to credit-hour weighting. Practical exams in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology are often easier to improve than theory. Use this calculator to identify which subject improvements will have the most impact on your final GPA.
